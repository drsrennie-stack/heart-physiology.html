Hemodynamic Formula Reference
Parameter Formula Normal Adult Range
Cardiac Output (CO)CO = HR × SV4–8 L/min
Cardiac Index (CI)CI = CO / BSA2.5–4.0 L/min/m²
Stroke Volume (SV)SV = EDV − ESV60–100 mL/beat
Ejection Fraction (EF)EF = (SV / EDV) × 100≥ 55%
Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP)MAP = DBP + ⅓(SBP − DBP)70–100 mmHg
Pulse Pressure (PP)PP = SBP − DBP30–50 mmHg
Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR)SVR = [(MAP − CVP) / CO] × 80800–1200 dynes·s/cm⁵
Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR)PVR = [(mPAP − PCWP) / CO] × 8020–130 dynes·s/cm⁵
Shock Index (SI)SI = HR / SBP0.5–0.7
Cardiac Reserve (CR)CR = Max CO − Resting CO4–5× resting CO
Unit Reminders

HR = bpm | SV = mL/beat | CO = L/min (convert SV: mL ÷ 1000 = L) | BSA = m² | CI = L/min/m²

MAP, CVP, mPAP, PCWP = mmHg | SVR, PVR = dynes·s/cm⁵ | EF = % | PP = mmHg

The ×80 in SVR/PVR converts from mmHg·min/L to dynes·s/cm⁵ (standard resistance units).

Physiology Reasoning Reference · What Causes Each Value to Rise or Fall?
Parameter ↑ Increases with… ↓ Decreases with…
Heart Rate (HR) Fever, sympathetic activation (NE/epi, β1), exercise, pain, anxiety, hypoxia, hyperthyroidism, hypovolemia (reflex tachycardia), caffeine, atropine Parasympathetic/vagal tone (ACh, M2), beta-blockers, hypothermia, hypothyroidism, endurance athletes (high vagal tone), digoxin, Ca²⁺ channel blockers
Stroke Volume (SV) Increased preload (more venous return/EDV = Frank-Starling), increased contractility (sympathetic, epi/NE, dobutamine), decreased afterload (vasodilation, ACE inhibitors), exercise Decreased preload (hemorrhage, dehydration, diuretics), decreased contractility (MI, acidosis, anoxia, cardiomyopathy, beta-blockers, Ca²⁺ channel blockers), increased afterload (hypertension, aortic stenosis, high SVR)
Cardiac Output (CO) Exercise (HR + SV both rise), fever (HR rises via SA node), sympathetic stimulation (epi/NE), pregnancy, hyperthyroidism, sepsis (early), inotropes (dobutamine) Heart failure (decreased SV), any shock state, beta-blockers, severe bradycardia, hypothermia, cardiac tamponade, extreme tachycardia (paradox: too-fast HR shortens fill time → decreased EDV → decreased SV)
Ejection Fraction (EF) Sympathetic stimulation (NE/epi increase Ca²⁺ entry, stronger cross-bridge cycling), exercise, positive inotropes (dobutamine, digoxin), reduced afterload (vasodilators, ACE inhibitors) Myocardial infarction (necrotic scar = lost contractile muscle), dilated cardiomyopathy, acidosis, hypoxia, severe tachycardia, high afterload (aortic stenosis, uncontrolled hypertension)
MAP Increased SVR (vasoconstriction from catecholamines, Ang II, vasopressors), increased CO (exercise, fluid loading), fluid retention (aldosterone, ADH), pain/anxiety (sympathetic surge) Decreased SVR (sepsis, anaphylaxis, ACE inhibitors, nitroglycerin), decreased CO (heart failure, hemorrhage, tamponade), diuretics (decreased volume decreases venous return and CO)
Pulse Pressure (PP) Increased SV (exercise, aortic regurgitation = blood leaks back so DBP falls sharply), arterial stiffness (aging, arteriosclerosis, SBP spikes higher), hyperthyroidism, anemia, AV fistula Decreased SV (cardiac tamponade, cardiogenic shock, aortic stenosis, hypovolemia), tachycardia (short diastole = decreased EDV = decreased SV = narrower PP)
SVR Sympathetic vasoconstriction (NE, α1 receptors), angiotensin II (RAAS activation), vasopressors (norepinephrine, vasopressin), hypothermia, hypovolemia (reflex vasoconstriction to protect BP) Vasodilation from sepsis (nitric oxide, cytokines), anaphylaxis (histamine), neurogenic shock (lost sympathetic tone), ACE inhibitors, nitrates, calcium channel blockers, heat
PVR Hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction (unique to lungs — low O₂ constricts, opposite of systemic), pulmonary hypertension, COPD, pulmonary fibrosis, recurrent PE, acidosis, hypothermia Supplemental oxygen, pulmonary vasodilators (sildenafil, inhaled nitric oxide, prostacyclins), correction of acidosis, rewarming the patient
Shock Index (SI) Increased HR (compensatory tachycardia from hemorrhage, sepsis, pain, anxiety raises the numerator), decreased SBP (any cause of hypotension — shock, vasodilation, pump failure — lowers the denominator) Restored blood pressure via fluids, vasopressors, or blood products (SBP rises = denominator rises), controlled heart rate by treating the underlying cause (HR falls = numerator falls)
